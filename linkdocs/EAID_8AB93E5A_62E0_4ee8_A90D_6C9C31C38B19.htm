Description by WHO


Symptoms and Signs: In the ICD-11, no distinction will be imposed between signs and symptoms as their differentiation may be confusing in care practice or medical records. Symptoms generally refer to indication of a disease or disorder especially experienced by a patient as sensations which may show that they are experiencing a health related problem. Signs refer to evidence recognized by the patient, doctor, or others of a health-related problem using hands, ears, eyes and nose and some diagnostic instruments.  Enlisting of signs and symptoms ensures a level of quality when identifying the appropriate ICD category.   Knowledge from health sciences will be elaborated by the TAGs as defining characteristics of the ICD categories. In most instances specialty adaptations and WHO Guidelines refer to this information.   The potential value sets include SNOMED-CT and LOINC.

Description by Extended Model

Sensations that the patients themselves can feel that are evidence that they are experiencing a health related problem (symptom)
Evidence recognized by the patient, doctor, or others of a health-related problem using hands, ears, eyes and nose (sign)
No Distinction will be imposed between signs and symptoms
Ensures a common level of quality when identifying the appropriate entity
