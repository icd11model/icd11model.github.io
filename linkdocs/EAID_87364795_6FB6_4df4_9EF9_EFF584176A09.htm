Description by WHO


Treatment /Management: Definition of a disease (e.g. Title and Textual Definition) may sometimes be related to its treatment (e.g. Insulin Resistant Diabetes) or refer to Reason for Encounter (e.g. vaccination, dosing).  In addition, various WHO programmes promote and practice coupling diagnosis with management (such as Integrated Management of Childhood Illness, Integrated Management of Adult Illness) as in common health services use of classification is strongly associated with management guidelines.  Similar approaches have been taken by certain National Guidelines and WHO is requested to link the ICD categories to recommended interventions.  A formal mechanism to enable this linkage or articulation is therefore required.  Current ICD versions in certain specialty adaptations already make references to treatment and other management interventions. It will also enable linkages to International Classification of Health Interventions or its national equivalents to ensure quality for better health information systems including casemix.  Potential value sets include International Nonproprietary Names, WHO Drug Dictionary, Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical (ATC) Classification System and other terminologies such as SNOMED-CT.
