Description by WHO


Temporal Relations: Characterization of the onset, duration or course of a disease/health condition has been traditionally used for classifying its subtypes.  ICD-11 attempts to formally represent the knowledge about the temporal relations of a category.  Temporal patterns indicate the duration of ICD entity which may be useful for coding and differential diagnostics. Nevertheless, the traditional terminology used for this parameter has been quite heterogeneous and non-standard.  Definitions of acute, subacute, chronic or other qualifiers have been varied across disease groups. It is required to express such terms in absolute time units and patterns of course if possible to be used as a formal operational definition by the TAG HIM and other TAGs.
