Description by WHO


Body Systems (Physiology):  The ICD has traditionally used body systems as an organizing principle. It is necessary to denote each category to one of the body systems.  Traditional divisions of body systems may be seen as arbitrary as many body parts can be a part of multiple body systems (for example tonsils refer to upper respiratory tract as because of tonsil infections although they are part of immunological system). Despite this type of confusion, such characteristics serve to facilitate the creation of meaningful subsets for coding and for analysis. This characterization must be in line with systems currently listed in the ICD-10 to ensure compatibility.  Topic Advisory Groups will make the recommendation for this assignment.
