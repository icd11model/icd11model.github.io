Description by WHO


Causal Agents:  refer to entities identified as the responsible for the etiology of the health condition, such as the bacterium for infections, chemicals for a particular injury. Given the multiple causality patterns, multiple entries are possible for any given category.   ICD has traditionally placed the external causes of injury in Chapter 20.  In addition, microbial agents, known genetic causes such as chromosomal anomalies have been traditionally listed.  


Description by Extended Model

A physical entity that is the basis for health-related problems at the end of the etiological chain: bacterium for infections, chemicals for a particular injury
