Frankly, this is the least developed of all the diagrams in our new three layer model.

Simplistically, it is supposed to be a catalog or dictionary of terminology reference

information, such as elements of SNOMED, elements of Hugo, ICF, or other granular

terminologies. It is in this domain that traditional ontological models (such as the assertion that

a myocardial infarction is death of the myocyte due to ischemia) would exist. It is from this

source that all other layers derive their ontological basis. That being said, I think the model in its

present form is grossly underspecified and somewhat simplistic. I think if we have

improvements, they would occur mostly in this layer. Bedirhan has made very clear that ICD 11

should have this layer independent of other ontologies, and it should not be ceded entirely to

terminology such as SNOMED. However, it would be highly desirable that elements in this

layer should correspond closely if not exactly to elements in SNOMED.


Note by Alan Rector:


The ontology layer is serving two functions:


a) As a controlled vocabulary for the category layer.
b) As a the source of necessary conditions that define entities.

The principle should be that each piece of information should reside in exactly one place, 
so that for maintenance it can be changed in exactly one place.

This means that for those entities that do have a representation in the Ontology level, the
the representation to them at other levels should be tightly tied to the ontology level. 
Eg.  If there is "Stomach cancer" in the ontology level, then "Stomach cancer" - or whatever
corresponding entity in the Category and Linearization levels should reference it and use its
presentation object and mappings by default. If they need to over-ride them, they can, but to take
a trivial example, I should have to translate "Stomach cancer" to "Cancer de L'estomach" (or whatever is the correct French) in only one place, and that would normally be the Presentation object on "Stomach Cancer" in the Ontology layer.  

Ontological definitions:

If we look at SNOMED, we will find "Stomach Cancer" as Carcinoma qualified by site Stomach. [1] 
That's at least a basic definition in formal terms.   Stomach cancer with site Brain doesn't make sense. 

These aren't diagnostic criteria, they are what something is, or at least a part of the definition. 

Actually, of course, this is an inadequate definition.  Stomach cancer is actually a carcinoma arising from the mucosa of the stomach.  Primary stomach cancer is also sited in the stomach.  Metastatic stomach cancer can be sited anywhere.  

From the definition of stomach cancer we can determine both that it is a disease of the Stomach and hence of the GI Tract.  From the fact that it is a carcinoma we can identify that it is a solid tumour, and from this that it is a solid tumour of the GI tract - if anybody is interested which from some recent statistics I've seen they might be. 

From this we can also determine with certainty, if it becomes necessary to differentiate them, that 
Carcinoma of the Antrim of the stomach is a kind of Carcinoma of the Stomach.

Not all diseases have such clearcut defiitions.  Myocardial Infarct is a bit fuzzy. Rheumatoid Arthritis or Diabetes Type I probably only have very partial definitions, perhaps only that they are a "Rhematoid disease" and a "Metabolic disease" respectively.

However, these things are all things that are true of ALL Stomach cancers, Gastric Cancers, Rheumatoid Arthritis.


ONTOLOGY LAYER, DLS AND BINDING TO SNOMED AND MAPPINGS

If we say that the ontology level should be an "ontology" in the DL sense, then
several things follow.

i)           The hierarchy is based on necessary subsumption, i.e. all members of a subclass are necessarily members of the superclass.

ii)          I am not sure how easily OWL/DLs fits into the Lexgrid model or whether we need an indirection mechanism.

iii)          I would be in favour of "borrowing" snomed defintions but being able
             a) to enhance them as we require - hoping that SNOMED will accept the enhancements where they
                 are in SNOMED's scope.
             b) Explicitly to reject them where they present problems.

To say that the ontology layer follows a DL does not mean that the DL section has to be built first.
It is probably more sensible to derive both from from user-oriented model adapted to the Wiki.

Where we do use an external ontology, we should of couse retain the reference to it, including versioning information, and will have to have a policy on mutual update. 

For SNOMEDI would hope that  our definitions would always be strictly richer than theirs - i.e. supersets - unless we had submitted a request for change or indication of probable error.  

For things like HUGO, the most that we can probably take are the list of identifiers and the hierarchy.  (i don't know whether even the hierarchy is reliable formally) In that case we will need to be able to cope with minimal information and enrich later.
