Note by Alan Rector about why using Broader/Narrower:

This is more than a labelling issue. It is highly substantive.

* Superclass/Subclass means that every member of the superclass is a member of the

subclass. This is a logical relation with logical criteria.

* Broader_than/Narrower_than is a linguistic, or more properly information retrieval

term, that means that the broader items should be retrieved, or browsed to

when the narrow terms are requested.

Example. In a thesaurus, "Aortic valve" might be "narrower than" "Heart". However,

"Aortic Valve" is not a subclass/kind of "heart". Librarians and information retrieval

specialists use "Broader than"/"narrower than" specifically to avoid the implications of

Superclass/Subclass and to emphasise that the relation should not be used for inheritance

or other logical functions, but only for navigation and browsing. Everything true of all

members of a superclass is true of all members of a subclass. There is no implication that

things true of members of a broader class are also true of the narrower class.
