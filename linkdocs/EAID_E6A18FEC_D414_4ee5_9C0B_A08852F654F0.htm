As I said, this is what I'm now calling the Category Layer, as evident by the ICD 11 category in the center. This I see as the core of the classification, against which the subtype-supertype hierarchy would be built. The related classes of Presentation, Comment, and Definition, are directly derived from the LexGrid model, and accommodate virtually all of the lexical, natural language (such as French or Japanese) manifestations. What is strikingly new and different is that the historical clinical characteristic, which is at the bottom of that particular model, is now linked to a Category exclusively through Clinical Criteria. This means, that if a clinical characteristic (such as anatomic site, genomic underpinning, or related characteristic) is not manifest in a clinical criteria, it has no role in the model. This is made explicit by the cardinality. Closely related to this are the Sanctioning Rules, which we chose to attach to clinical criteria. This we borrowed directly from Stephanie and Can's previous representations. I think this is the core of what I see ICD 11 being, with few restrictions on the subtype-supertype hierarchy (meaning that multiple inheritance is permitted in this layer). Specifically, these not need to be mutually exclusive at this phase of ICD 11 manifestation. Notions such as mutually exclusive and for that matter exhaustive can and should occur at the serialization layer.

THESAURUS / BROWSING / SEARCH  / LAYER - a fourth layer? / A replacement for the Index.

I understand completely why Chris wanted to allow broader-than/narrower than rather than pure subclass links in the Category layer.  They are much easier for people to use for browsing and searching for material than a strict class hierarchy.  

I think the problem is that we want the Category layer to do too many things.  For some of them we need a "proper" classification hierarchy.  For others we need a broader-than/narrower-than thesaurus, probably with lots of bells and whistles for helping people search it efficiently.

This is how I would replace the existing Index.  My preference would be to do this with SKOS, as it provides a well thought out and standardised vocabulary for Thesauri, but there are others.   We have a student working on OWL<--> SKOS projections.  We have a very similar structure in one of our commercial projects.  If we do this, we can be entirely clear that the purpose of this tool is for human browsing and searching.  The entities should all come from the other hierarchies, but they could be supplemented with Presentation_entities arbitrarily.   Ideally, there would be a defined projection, e.g. that broader-than was the union of kind-of, part-of and causes (so that Diabetes is broader than Diabetic Nephropathy)
